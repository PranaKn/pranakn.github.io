  • FCC warns of radio equipment hijacking for fake EAS tones: The **US Federal Communications Commission (FCC)** issued an advisory warning broadcasters about a string of cyber intrusions where hackers hijacked US radio transmission equipment to air **fake Emergency Alert System (EAS) tones** and offensive material. The attacks primarily targeted unsecured **Barix network audio devices**, reconfiguring them to broadcast attacker-controlled audio streams.   • FBI and Amazon warn of holiday Account Takeover (ATO) surge: Amazon and the FBI have issued warnings about a major surge in **Account Takeover (ATO) fraud** targeting holiday shoppers, with losses exceeding $262 million reported to the FBI this year. Attack vectors include phishing texts, calls, look-alike websites, SEO poisoning, and the use of the **Matrix Push C2 platform** to abuse browser push notifications for phishing. More info
  • CISA warns of commercial spyware attacks on Signal/WhatsApp users: CISA has issued an advisory warning that cybercriminals and state-backed groups are using **commercial spyware** to compromise the smartphones of "high-value" targets (political, military, government personnel). Techniques include tricking users into scanning malicious QR codes to link accounts or exploiting sophisticated **zero-click vulnerabilities**, such as the **LANDFALL exploit** (CVE-2025-21042) in a Samsung Android library triggered via a malformed WhatsApp image. More info
