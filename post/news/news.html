  • Bloody Wolf expands Java-based RAT campaign: The threat actor **Bloody Wolf**, active since late 2023, has expanded its spear-phishing campaign across Central Asia, targeting finance, government, and IT sectors in **Kyrgyzstan** since June 2025 and **Uzbekistan** as of October 2025. The group impersonates government ministries to deliver **NetSupport RAT**, leveraging social engineering. The attack chain uses a malicious **Java Archive (JAR) loader** to fetch the payload, establishing persistence via a scheduled task, Registry key, and a Startup batch script. The Uzbekistan campaign notably used **geofencing** to target local users only. More info
  • Scattered Lapsus$ Hunters target Zendesk users: The cybercrime alliance **Scattered Lapsus$ Hunters** is suspected of launching a new extortion campaign targeting **Zendesk users**, leveraging more than 40 typosquatted domains. The domains host fake single sign-on pages for credential harvesting or are used to submit malicious helpdesk tickets. Researchers warn that these weaponized tickets could lead to dropping **Remote Access Trojans** onto helpdesk agents' machines, enabling lateral movement and data theft. This strategy follows their pattern of abusing SaaS platforms. More info

  • FCC warns of radio equipment hijacking for fake EAS tones: The **US Federal Communications Commission (FCC)** issued an advisory warning broadcasters about cyber intrusions where hackers hijacked US radio transmission equipment to air **fake Emergency Alert System (EAS) tones** and offensive material. The attacks primarily targeted unsecured **Barix network audio devices**, reconfiguring them to broadcast attacker-controlled audio streams. The FCC urged broadcasters to implement immediate security measures like installing patches, replacing default passwords, and placing EAS/Barix equipment behind firewalls and VPNs. More info
  • FBI and Amazon warn of holiday Account Takeover (ATO) surge: Amazon and the FBI have issued warnings about a major surge in **Account Takeover (ATO) fraud** targeting holiday shoppers, with losses exceeding $262 million reported to the FBI this year. Threat actors leverage **social engineering** and **credential stuffing** to hijack accounts. Attack vectors include phishing texts, calls, look-alike websites, SEO poisoning, and the use of the **Matrix Push C2 platform** to abuse browser push notifications for phishing. More info
  • CISA warns of commercial spyware attacks on Signal/WhatsApp users: CISA has issued an advisory warning that cybercriminals and state-backed groups are using **commercial spyware** to compromise the smartphones of "high-value" targets. The attacks bypass end-to-end encryption by **compromising the device itself**. Techniques include tricking users into scanning malicious QR codes or exploiting sophisticated **zero-click vulnerabilities**, such as the **LANDFALL exploit** (CVE-2025-21042) in a Samsung Android library triggered via a malformed WhatsApp image. More info
