  • DKnife toolkit targets edge devices: Cisco Talos uncovered DKnife, a China-nexus AitM framework active since 2019. Using seven Linux implants, it performs deep packet inspection on routers to hijack app updates and deliver ShadowPad/DarkNimbus backdoors. It specifically targets Chinese-speaking users, disrupts security traffic with TCP RST packets, and shares infrastructure with WizardNet campaigns. More info
  • Global "Shadow Campaigns" by TGR-STA-1030: Unit 42 detailed a massive espionage operation (aka UNC6619) that compromised 70 organizations across 37 countries. Using the "Diaoyu" loader and a custom eBPF rootkit called "ShadowGuard," the group hides kernel-level processes to maintain long-term access. The actor focuses on government ministries and regions experiencing significant geopolitical shifts. More info
  • Signal phishing targeting high-ranking officials: German agencies (BfV and BSI) issued a joint advisory regarding state-sponsored phishing on Signal. Attackers impersonate "Signal Support" to trick political and military figures into sharing SMS verification codes or scanning malicious QR codes to hijack accounts. While no malware is used, the campaign risks compromising sensitive professional networks. More info
